We're so back (we were never here before)

Hey. Mr. Brumous here.

I've been a gamer my whole life, and somewhere in the back of my head the thought of making a game has always been floating around. Never acted on it though — until recently, when I was doing that thing where you hop from one two-week hobby to the next, never finishing anything, just vibing. 3D modelling for a bit, some world building, dabbling in Photoshop, a week of convincing myself I'd learn music production. You know the drill.

And then it hit me — what if I just mashed all of these scattered hobby phases into one project? Enter Renascent. A game I'm making, apparently.

Small caveat: I had never touched game development before starting this. My technical background is roughly "I kind of know what HTML is." After a quick research spiral, Unreal Engine 5 seemed like the obvious pick — the amount of tutorials and guides on YouTube alone is genuinely staggering, and it seems well suited for exactly the kind of game rattling around in my head.

Speaking of which.

I'm aware that every experienced game developer gives the same advice to newcomers: start small, make something simple, your first project will be bad anyway so keep it short. That's probably correct advice. I'm choosing to ignore it.

Here's the thing — I'm not doing this to ship a fully polished game, and I'm definitely not trying to pivot into a game dev career. I'm doing this because I'm genuinely curious whether I can pull it off, and because "something small and simple" sounds incredibly boring. What doesn't sound boring? Complex immersive worlds. Combat that actually requires skill. A deep character progression system. A meta progression loop that's punishing but keeps you coming back.

So naturally, I decided my first game would be a souls-like roguelite action RPG. Yes, I know. Very original concept for an indie dev in 2026. I'm aware. Don't @ me.

Renascent is set in an original fantasy world drawing from northern pagan mythology and folklore — more ancient nature spirits, shamans, and curses than anything you'd recognise from popular media. More on the actual game over on the Renascent page.

Updates here will be sporadic at best. I'm also finishing up university and working full time, so this is strictly a hobby project running on whatever energy is left at the end of the day. But if you're curious enough to stick around — welcome. Should be a ride.
